Background

Researchers in the medical field often have little experience in UI and UX design. Often times, this leads to underdeveloped front ends for web apps connected to research papers. This in turn can lead to lower understanding of the reserach material by others, and can hinder the progress of collaborative research. By creating an easy to use frontend framework, researchers will be able to plug in their already made functions into an aesthetically pleasing template with minimal effort, allowing them to focus on their own work.

An example of a web app created with the framework.

Development

I decided to focus heavily on the ease of transition between local functions to server hosted web apps. By using Shiny, R programs can be converted into a UI/request based application, though the UI needs to be developed further. By implementing Shiny functions into the framework, need for redundant documentation was reduced, and by preimplementing as many of the "basics" of web apps as possible, the framework can quickly be used without need for consideration of the more complex, but common, intricacies of online hosted applications. Furthermore, easy access to commonly changed variables (such as colors) was heavily considered, and were made easy to access when using the framework.

A near blank template of the framework being filled.

Conclusion

With this framework, researchers can quickly develop applications without the need to consider the frontend while they conduct resaerch. Presets are a good way to increase usability of frameworks that may be too complicated otherwise.
