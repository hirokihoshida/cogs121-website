Hiroki Hoshida

FocusNow

Background

Many people often struggle with time management, especially in balancing their workload with breaks. In particular, people who need to adapt to a more rigorous schedule (high school/college freshmen, etc.) need to develop good work habits quickly or they will cement bad habits that become increasingly harder to shake off as time goes on. Many students are never taught proper time management techniques, and have to go off their own experiences. A webapp that creates a strict work/break micro schedule based on the user’s overall schedule can be helpful in developing these habits.

FocusNow Main Screen.

Research

First, I needed to observe how people studied in the first place. I observed multiple peoples’ study habits, such as how they rationed out their breaks (long breaks between each task, short breaks during tasks, etc.), and surveyed how productive they felt after different kinds of studying methods. Second, I researched various time management techniques, and tried various methods to see which felt most effective, and how they can be improved.

Task creation page for FocusNow.

Development

The main focus was ease of integration into already existing schedules. There was no need to rebuild an entire schedule/calendar app, but just the need for a schedule micromanager. I decided that users should Input tasks already decided on earlier, then FocusNow can then automatically section them off into small pieces. Another focus was the ease of understanding the usage of the app as quickly as possible. In order to do this, FocusNow automatically does functions such as splitting tasks as soon as user inputs them, and sets default values (that user can change) that allows for optimal use.

Task page with automatic task rationing and ordering.

Conclusions

Early focus on study habits were important

  • Don’t try to reshape habits entirely, allow for integration and a more subtle adaptation for users
Cut down on unnecessary features
  • Most people already have a calendar app of their choice, don’t try to force users to replace items in their routine if you want to introduce something new.
